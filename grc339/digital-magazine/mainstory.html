Hans Flick

Hansi Flick has been appointed as the new manager of FC Barcelona, succeeding Xavi Hernández. Flick, who is 59 years old, signed a contract that will keep him at the club until June 2026. He comes to Barcelona with a notable managerial pedigree, having previously led Bayern Munich to a historic sextuple in 2020, which included winning the Bundesliga, DFB-Pokal, UEFA Champions League, DFL-Supercup, UEFA Super Cup, and FIFA Club World Cup​.

At Barcelona, Flick is expected to bring his tactical acumen and experience to rejuvenate the team following a disappointing season under Xavi, who was dismissed after a second-place finish in La Liga. Flick expressed his excitement and honor at joining Barcelona, highlighting it as a dream opportunity to manage such a prestigious club​.

Julián Araujo is set to return to Barcelona after a successful loan spell with Las Palmas. The 22-year-old right-back joined Las Palmas on loan for the 2023-2024 season to gain more playing experience and has made a notable impact. During his time at Las Palmas, Araujo featured regularly, making 17 appearances in La Liga and scoring his first league goal. His performances have been impressive, leading to his anticipated return to Barcelona​.

Araujo's stint at Las Palmas was intended to help him adapt to the style of play and intensity of European football, aligning with Barcelona's strategic planning for his development. Barcelona sees him as a potential replacement for Joao Cancelo, whose future at the club is uncertain due to financial considerations. With Sergiño Dest likely staying at PSV Eindhoven and Sergi Roberto's contract ending, Araujo is positioned to compete for the right-back spot at Barcelona in the upcoming season​.

Lamine Yamal

Lamine Yamal, a 16-year-old sensation from Barcelona, has been making waves as one of the most promising young talents in football. His exceptional performances for Barcelona have earned him a spot in Spain's squad for Euro 2024, highlighting his rapid rise and significant impact at such a young age​. Yamal is also leading the race for the 2024 Golden Boy Award, which is awarded to the best under-21 player in European leagues.

His outstanding play has positioned him ahead of other top young players in the rankings​. Known for his creativity, left-footed skill, and ability to make a difference in games, Yamal is frequently compared to Lionel Messi. Despite his youth, he has already become a key player for Barcelona, with coach Xavi acknowledging his immense potential and the challenge of managing his playing time to avoid burnout​.

La Liga

Barcelona secured the La Liga title in the 2008-2009 season on May 16, 2009, when their closest rivals, Real Madrid, lost to Villarreal. As a result, Barcelona clinched the title mathematically without needing to play their own match. Barcelona themselves played against Mallorca the next day, on May 17, 2009, and the match ended in a 2-1 loss for Barcelona. Despite this defeat, they had already secured the La Liga title.

Copa Del Rey

Barcelona won the Copa del Rey in 2009 by defeating Athletic Bilbao in the final. The match took place on May 13, 2009, at the Mestalla Stadium in Valencia. Barcelona emerged victorious with a 4-1 win over Athletic Bilbao, securing their 25th Copa del Rey title.

Champion League

Barcelona won the UEFA Champions League in 2009 by defeating Manchester United in the final. The match took place on May 27, 2009, at the Stadio Olimpico in Rome. Barcelona emerged victorious with a 2-0 win, with goals from Samuel Eto'o and Lionel Messi, securing their third Champions League title.

Club World Cup

Barcelona won the FIFA Club World Cup in 2009 by defeating Estudiantes de La Plata in the final. The match took place on December 19, 2009, in Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates. Barcelona won 2-1 after extra time, with goals from Pedro and Lionel Messi, securing their first Club World Cup title.

Supercopa de Espana

Barcelona won the Supercopa de España in 2009 by defeating Athletic Bilbao in a two-legged final. The first leg took place on August 16, 2009, at San Mamés, where Barcelona emerged victorious with a 2-1 win. In the second leg, held on August 23, 2009, at Camp Nou, Barcelona secured a dominant 3-0 victory. With an aggregate score of 5-1, Barcelona claimed the Supercopa de España title, adding another trophy to their remarkable 2009 collection.
